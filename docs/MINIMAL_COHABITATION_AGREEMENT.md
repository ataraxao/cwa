# MINIMAL COHABITATION AGREEMENT
## (OFF-POOL RESIDENCE AND HOUSEHOLD LOGISTICS ONLY)
*Conceptual template. Not legal or tax advice.*

> **IMPORTANT NOTICE.** This template is intended for educational and drafting-reference purposes only. It is not legal advice, tax advice, or financial advice. Laws governing cohabitation, occupancy, reimbursement, property rights, arbitration, and equitable claims vary significantly by jurisdiction. Each Party should consult independent legal counsel before signing.

> **DESIGN INTENT.** This Agreement is intentionally narrow. It is designed to govern only off-pool residence, household cost allocation, household goods, and move-out logistics. It is not intended to govern any ring-fenced investment pool, Vehicle LLC, Vehicle Account, Allocation Percentage, B Distribution Ledger, Liquidity Event, Joint Pro Rata Release, or Termination unwind governed by any separate Cooperative Wealth Governance Agreement (“CWA”).

## ARTICLE II — CWA SEPARATION; NON-INCORPORATION; EXCLUDED PROPERTY

### 2.1 Separate CWA
The Parties acknowledge that they have entered, or may enter, a separate Cooperative Wealth Governance Agreement (the “**CWA**”) governing only a defined investment pool and related mechanics (collectively, the “**CWA Property and Mechanics**”).

### 2.2 No Incorporation
Except for boundary-setting and exclusion purposes only:
- the **CWA is not incorporated into this Agreement**; and
- this **Agreement is not incorporated into the CWA**.

### 2.3 Excluded Property and Excluded Rights
The following are expressly excluded from this Agreement:

(a) the Vehicle LLC;  
(b) the Vehicle Account;  
(c) all cash, securities, proceeds, distributions, or transfers associated with the Vehicle Account;  
(d) any Allocation Percentage, B Distribution Ledger entry, Liquidity Event right, Joint Pro Rata Release right, or Termination unwind right under the CWA; and  
(e) any claim to interpret, vary, expand, offset, supplement, or recharacterize the CWA Property and Mechanics.

### 2.4 No Interpretive Use Across Agreements
To the maximum extent permitted by law:
- this Agreement shall not be used to interpret, supplement, vary, or contradict the CWA;
- the CWA shall not be used to interpret, supplement, vary, or contradict this Agreement; and
- no payment pattern, side letter, oral statement, informal message, or relationship-adjacent understanding under one agreement shall be used to reframe the other.

### 2.5 No Claim to CWA Property
No provision of this Agreement creates any ownership, reimbursement, lien, constructive-trust theory, offset right, support right, equitable claim, or participation right in any CWA Property or Mechanics, except to the extent a non-waivable right under applicable law expressly requires otherwise.

## ARTICLE V — HOUSEHOLD GOODS; PERSONAL PROPERTY

### 5.2 Individually Purchased Household Goods
A household good purchased solely by one Party with that Party’s own funds shall be presumed to belong solely to that Party unless otherwise agreed in writing.

## ARTICLE VI — NO COMMINGLING WITH CWA PROPERTY

### 6.1 No Use of CWA Rails for Household Expenses
Neither Party shall intentionally use:
- the Vehicle LLC,
- the Vehicle Account,
- or any CWA distribution rail

### 6.2 No Reframing of CWA
Nothing in this Agreement shall describe or imply that the CWA provides:
- security,
- support,
- shared household reimbursement,
- compensation,
- or breakup protection in exchange for cohabitation.

### 7.1 Notice of Move-Out

### 7.2 Household Reconciliation
Within **[__] days** after move-out, the Parties shall attempt in good faith to resolve:
- final shared utility balances,
- final grocery/household reimbursements,
- return of keys/access devices,

- create a right to freeze, invade, offset, or reallocate CWA Property,
- or alter the allocation, liquidity, ledger, or unwind mechanics of the CWA.

### 10.3 Amendments
Any amendment to this Agreement must be in a signed writing by both Parties.

No amendment to this Agreement shall affect the CWA unless:
(a) it expressly states that it is intended to affect the CWA,  
(b) it specifically identifies the provision(s) of the CWA to be affected, and  
(c) it satisfies any amendment formalities required by the CWA.

### 10.4 Severability
If any provision of this Agreement is held invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions remain in effect to the maximum extent permitted by law. The Parties request narrowing or reformation where available to preserve the separation between this Agreement and the CWA.
